<h3 id="pointed-sets:subsection-free-pointed-sets" class="tex-subsection"><span data-tag="00BW">5.4.1</span> Free Pointed Sets</h3>
<p>
 Let $X$ be a set. <div class="env-definition-header"><a class="environment-identifier" href="/tag/00BX">Definition <span data-tag="00BX">5.4.1.1.1</span><span class="triangleright"> ▶ </span><span class="named">Free Pointed Sets</span></a></div>
<article class="env-definition" id="00BX">
  <p>
    The <strong>free pointed set on $X$</strong> is the pointed set $\smash {X^{+}}$ consisting of: </p>   <p>
    <ul>
  <li    class="enumerate-item" >
<span class="counter">
<span class="counter-link" href="/tag/a0000066913"><span class="counter-inner-no-tag">•</span></span></span>
<span class="item-content"><p>
<em>The Underlying Set. </em>The set $X^{+}$ defined by<span class="footnote" id="fnmark-a0000066918"><sup>1</sup></span>
</p>
<div class="equation">
  \begin{align*}  X^{+} & \mathrel {\smash {\overset {\mathclap {\scriptscriptstyle \text{def}}}=}}X\mathchoice {\mathbin {\textstyle \coprod }}{\mathbin {\textstyle \coprod }}{\mathbin {\scriptstyle \textstyle \coprod }}{\mathbin {\scriptscriptstyle \textstyle \coprod }}\mathrm{pt}\\ & \mathrel {\smash {\overset {\mathclap {\scriptscriptstyle \text{def}}}=}}X\mathchoice {\mathbin {\textstyle \coprod }}{\mathbin {\textstyle \coprod }}{\mathbin {\scriptstyle \textstyle \coprod }}{\mathbin {\scriptscriptstyle \textstyle \coprod }}\left\{ \star \right\} . \end{align*}
</div>
</span>
</li><li    class="enumerate-item" >
<span class="counter">
<span class="counter-link" href="/tag/a0000067007"><span class="counter-inner-no-tag">•</span></span></span>
<span class="item-content"><p>
<em>The Basepoint. </em>The element $\star $ of $X^{+}$. 
</p>
</span>
</li>
</ul></p> 
   <div class="local-footnotes">
    <hr />     <ol class="footnotes"><li id="fn-a0000066918">
         <sup>1</sup><em>Further Notation: </em>We sometimes write $\star _{X}$ for the basepoint of $X^{+}$ for clarity, specially when there are multiple free pointed sets involved in the current discussion. </li>
    </ol>

<div class="env-proof-header"><strong>Proof of <a href="/tag/00BY" data-tag="00BY">Proposition 5.4.1.1.2</a>.</strong></div>
<article class="env-proof">
    <p>
       <div class="proofbox"><a href="/tag/00BZ" data-tag="00BZ">Item 1</a>: Functoriality</div>We claim that $\webleft (-\webright )^{+}$ is indeed a functor: 
    </p>
    <p>
      <ul>
  <li    class="enumerate-item" >
<span class="counter">
<span class="counter-link" href="/tag/a0000067495"><span class="counter-inner-no-tag">•</span></span></span>
<span class="item-content"><p>
<em>Preservation of Identities. </em>Let $X\in \operatorname {\mathrm{Obj}}\webleft (\mathsf{Sets}\webright )$. We have 
</p>
<div class="equation">
  \[  \operatorname {\mathrm{id}}^{+}_{X}\webleft (x\webright )\mathrel {\smash {\overset {\mathclap {\scriptscriptstyle \text{def}}}=}}\begin{cases}  x & \text{if $x\in X$,}\\  \star _{X} & \text{if $x=\star _{X}$,} \end{cases}  \]
</div>
<p>
for each $x\in X^{+}$, so $\operatorname {\mathrm{id}}^{+}_{X}=\operatorname {\mathrm{id}}_{X^{+}}$. 
</p>
</span>
</li><li    class="enumerate-item" >
<span class="counter">
<span class="counter-link" href="/tag/a0000067560"><span class="counter-inner-no-tag">•</span></span></span>
<span class="item-content"><p>
<em>Preservation of Composition. </em>Given morphisms of sets 
</p>
<div class="equation">
  \begin{align*}  f & \colon X \to Y,\\  g & \colon Y \to Z, \end{align*}
</div>
<p>
 we have 
</p>
<div class="equation">
  \begin{align*}  \webleft [g^{+}\circ f^{+}\webright ]\webleft (x\webright ) & \mathrel {\smash {\overset {\mathclap {\scriptscriptstyle \text{def}}}=}}g^{+}\webleft (f^{+}\webleft (x\webright )\webright )\\ & \mathrel {\smash {\overset {\mathclap {\scriptscriptstyle \text{def}}}=}}g^{+}\webleft (f\webleft (x\webright )\webright )\\ & \mathrel {\smash {\overset {\mathclap {\scriptscriptstyle \text{def}}}=}}g\webleft (f\webleft (x\webright )\webright )\\ & \mathrel {\smash {\overset {\mathclap {\scriptscriptstyle \text{def}}}=}}\webleft [g\circ f\webright ]^{+}\webleft (x\webright )\end{align*}
</div>
<p>
 for each $x\in X$ and 
</p>
<div class="equation">
  \begin{align*}  \webleft [g^{+}\circ f^{+}\webright ]\webleft (\star _{X}\webright ) & \mathrel {\smash {\overset {\mathclap {\scriptscriptstyle \text{def}}}=}}g^{+}\webleft (f^{+}\webleft (\star _{X}\webright )\webright )\\ & \mathrel {\smash {\overset {\mathclap {\scriptscriptstyle \text{def}}}=}}g^{+}\webleft (\star _{Y}\webright )\\ & \mathrel {\smash {\overset {\mathclap {\scriptscriptstyle \text{def}}}=}}\star _{Z}\\ & \mathrel {\smash {\overset {\mathclap {\scriptscriptstyle \text{def}}}=}}\webleft [g\circ f\webright ]^{+}\webleft (\star _{X}\webright ), \end{align*}
</div>
<p>
 so $\webleft (g\circ f\webright )^{+}=g^{+}\circ f^{+}$. 
</p>
</span>
</li>
</ul>
    </p>
    <p>
       This finishes the proof. 
    </p>

      <ul>
  <li    class="enumerate-item"     id="0227"
>
<span class="counter">
<span class="counter-link" href="/tag/0227"><span class="counter-inner-no-tag">•</span></span></span>
<span class="item-content"><p>
<em>Map I. </em>We define a map

<div class="equation">
  \[  \Phi _{X,Y}\colon \mathsf{Sets}_{*}\webleft (X^{+},Y\webright )\to \mathsf{Sets}\webleft (X,Y\webright ) \]
</div>
<p>
by sending a morphism of pointed sets 
</p>
<div class="equation">
  \[  \xi \colon \webleft (X^{+},\star _{X}\webright )\to \webleft (Y,y_{0}\webright ) \]
</div>
<p>
to the function
</p>
<div class="equation">
  \[  \xi ^{\dagger }\colon X\to Y \]
</div>
<p>
given by 
</p>
<div class="equation">
  \[  \xi ^{\dagger }\webleft (x\webright )\mathrel {\smash {\overset {\mathclap {\scriptscriptstyle \text{def}}}=}}\xi \webleft (x\webright ) \]
</div>
<p>
for each $x\in X$. 
</p>
</span>
</li><li    class="enumerate-item"     id="0228"
>
<span class="counter">
<span class="counter-link" href="/tag/0228"><span class="counter-inner-no-tag">•</span></span></span>
<span class="item-content"><p>
<em>Map II. </em>We define a map

</div>
<p>
given by sending a function $\xi \colon X\to Y$ to the morphism of pointed sets 
</p>
<div class="equation">
  \[  \xi ^{\dagger }\colon \webleft (X^{+},\star _{X}\webright )\to \webleft (Y,y_{0}\webright ) \]
</div>
<p>
defined by 
</p>
<div class="equation">
  \[  \xi ^{\dagger }\webleft (x\webright )\mathrel {\smash {\overset {\mathclap {\scriptscriptstyle \text{def}}}=}}\begin{cases}  \xi \webleft (x\webright ) &  \text{if $x\in X$,}\\  y_{0} &  \text{if $x=\star _{X}$} \end{cases}  \]
</div>
<p>
for each $x\in X^{+}$. 
</p>
</span>
</li><li    class="enumerate-item"     id="0229"
>
<span class="counter">
<span class="counter-link" href="/tag/0229"><span class="counter-inner-no-tag">•</span></span></span>
<span class="item-content"><p>
<em>Invertibility I. </em>Given a morphism of pointed sets 
</p>
<div class="equation">
  \[  \xi \colon \webleft (X^{+},\star _{X}\webright )\to \webleft (Y,y_{0}\webright ), \]
</div>
<p>
we have 
</p>
<div class="equation">
  \begin{align*}  \webleft [\Psi _{X,Y}\circ \Phi _{X,Y}\webright ]\webleft (\xi \webright ) & \mathrel {\smash {\overset {\mathclap {\scriptscriptstyle \text{def}}}=}}\Psi _{X,Y}\webleft (\Phi _{X,Y}\webleft (\xi \webright )\webright )\\ & = \Psi _{X,Y}\webleft (\xi ^{\dagger }\webright )\\ & \mathrel {\smash {\overset {\mathclap {\scriptscriptstyle \text{def}}}=}}\left[\mspace {-6mu}\left[x\mapsto {\begin{cases} \xi ^{\dagger }\webleft (x\webright )& \text{if $x\in X$}\\ y_{0}& \text{if $x=\star _{X}$}\end{cases}}\right]\mspace {-6mu}\right]\\ & = \left[\mspace {-6mu}\left[x\mapsto {\begin{cases} \xi \webleft (x\webright )& \text{if $x\in X$}\\ y_{0}& \text{if $x=\star _{X}$}\end{cases}}\right]\mspace {-6mu}\right]\\ & = \xi \\ & \mathrel {\smash {\overset {\mathclap {\scriptscriptstyle \text{def}}}=}}\webleft [\operatorname {\mathrm{id}}_{\mathsf{Sets}_{*}\webleft (X^{+},Y\webright )}\webright ]\webleft (\xi \webright ).\end{align*}
</div>
<p>
 Therefore we have 
</p>
<div class="equation">
  \[  \Psi _{X,Y}\circ \Phi _{X,Y}=\operatorname {\mathrm{id}}_{\mathsf{Sets}_{*}\webleft (X^{+},Y\webright )}. \]
</div>
</span>

<span class="counter">
<span class="counter-link" href="/tag/022A"><span class="counter-inner-no-tag">•</span></span></span>
<span class="item-content"><p>
<em>Invertibility II. </em>Given a map of sets $\xi \colon X\to Y$, we have 
</p>
<div class="equation">

 Therefore we have

<span class="counter-link" href="/tag/022B"><span class="counter-inner-no-tag">•</span></span></span>
<span class="item-content"><p>
<em>Naturality for $\Phi $, Part I. </em>We need to show that, given a morphism of pointed sets

 Therefore we have

<div class="equation">

    <p>

  <li    class="enumerate-item" >
<span class="counter">
<span class="counter-link" href="/tag/a0000068561"><span class="counter-inner-no-tag">•</span></span></span>
<span class="item-content"><p>

</p>
<div class="equation">
  \[  \webleft (-\webright )^{+,\mathchoice {\mathbin {\textstyle \coprod }}{\mathbin {\textstyle \coprod }}{\mathbin {\scriptstyle \textstyle \coprod }}{\mathbin {\scriptscriptstyle \textstyle \coprod }}}_{X,Y}\colon X^{+}\vee Y^{+}\overset {\scriptstyle \mathord {\sim }}{\dashrightarrow }\webleft (X\mathchoice {\mathbin {\textstyle \coprod }}{\mathbin {\textstyle \coprod }}{\mathbin {\scriptstyle \textstyle \coprod }}{\mathbin {\scriptscriptstyle \textstyle \coprod }}Y\webright )^{+}  \]
</div>
<p>
is given by
